Design Tips

Positioning an art piece.

An interesting piece of art is a good way to add a dose of color into a room and create that finished look. The trick to make it look nice is to position the piece at eye-level. If your room is a sitting or dining area, hang your art low enough to meet the eyes of those who are seated.

Expand your sight

Cultural influences are having a huge impact on design now, more than ever due to the advent of online shopping on a global scale. This has introduced us to surprising colors and patterns that we can try out just by using accessories. Using textiles is a great place to start.

All about the lightning

Choose appropriately scaled, relevant lighting. Lighting, more than any other element, asserts style, just like the perfect piece of jewelry defines the style of a little black dress. Don't be afraid to inject personality with a statement piece.

Attention to the mirrors

Mirrors can be used to make a small space feel larger. For any room with a more limited amount of natural light, mirrors placed directly across from the windows, will add instant light. Decorative mirrors can also be used in lieu of art to fill empty wall space. Large or small, mirrors add light and dimension to your living space.

Focal point to the rescue

If you're struggling with how to start decorating a room, finding its focal point is a good start. Many rooms have built-in focal points: a large window with a view, for example, or a fireplace.Once you find the focal point, decorate around it. Use its main color in elements throughout the rest of the room.
